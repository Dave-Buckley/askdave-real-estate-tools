Ask Dave Real Estate Tools – How It Works

A complete guide to every feature

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools v1.0.0 © 2026

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools runs quietly in your system tray and helps you do three things faster: call people, message people on WhatsApp, and send scripted messages. It works on top of whatever you already use – your CRM, your browser, Excel, emails, PDFs – anything where you can see a phone number on screen.

There are two main parts to the tool:

  • The Popup – a small floating bar that appears automatically when you copy a phone number
  • The Panel – a larger window you open from the system tray for templates and contact details

1. Automatic Phone Number Detection

Automatic

Copy a number, get instant actions

Whenever you copy a phone number to your clipboard (Ctrl+C), Ask Dave Real Estate Tools detects it automatically. A small popup appears next to your cursor with action buttons.

See a phone number → Select & copy it (Ctrl+C) → Popup appears at cursor → Click Dial or WhatsApp

Where it works: Everywhere. Browsers, CRM systems (Bayut, Property Finder, HubSpot, Salesforce), Excel, Word, PDFs, emails, WhatsApp Desktop, Outlook – if you can copy text, Ask Dave Real Estate Tools will detect the number.

What it detects:

FormatExampleDetected?

UAE mobile with country code+971501234567Yes

UAE mobile with spaces05 012 34567Yes

Smart number handling: Ask Dave Real Estate Tools automatically normalises every number to international format. So whether you copy "0501234567" or "00971501234567" or "+971 50 123 4567", it all becomes the same clean number.
Important: Ask Dave Real Estate Tools only triggers when it detects new clipboard content. If you copy the same phone number twice in a row, the popup won't appear a second time because the clipboard hasn't changed. To re-detect the same number, copy something else first (any text), then copy the phone number again.

2. The Popup – Quick Actions

Popup

Dial, WhatsApp, or send a script in one click

The popup is a compact floating bar that appears near your cursor when a phone number is detected. It has three buttons:

ButtonWhat it does
DialOpens the number in your phone's dialler via Phone Link. The number is pre-filled – it does not auto-call. You press the call button yourself.
WAClick to expand the script picker. Choose a script to send via WhatsApp with the message pre-filled, or choose "Open chat (no script)" to start a blank conversation.
XDismisses the popup.

Sending a script from the popup

Copy a phone number → Popup appears → Click WA button → Pick a script → WhatsApp opens with message ready

The script is pre-filled in the WhatsApp chat. You can edit it before sending or just hit Send.

Tip: The popup appears at your cursor, not in a fixed position. So if you're reading a contact list in your CRM, the buttons appear right where you're looking.

3. Click-to-Dial & Phone Link

Popup

How the Dial button works

When you click Dial on the popup or in the panel, Ask Dave Real Estate Tools sends the phone number to your phone's dialler via Microsoft Phone Link. The number is pre-filled and ready – you still press the green call button yourself. It will not auto-call anyone.

Click Dial → Phone Link receives number → Your phone's dialler opens → You press Call
Setup

Setting up Phone Link (one-time, Android + Windows)

If you haven't connected your phone yet, follow these steps once:

  1. On your PC, open the Start menu and search for "Phone Link". Open it.
  2. On your Android phone, install "Link to Windows" from the Google Play Store.
  3. In Phone Link on your PC, click Get started. A QR code appears on screen.
  4. Open the Link to Windows app on your phone and scan the QR code.
  5. Grant all permissions when asked – calls, contacts, messages, and notifications.
Important

Making calls go to your phone (not your PC speakers)

By default, Phone Link may try to ring through your PC speakers instead of your phone. To make calls go straight to your phone's dialler:

  1. Open Phone Link on your PC.
  2. Click the Settings gear icon (top-right corner).
  3. Go to Features.
  4. Under Calls, make sure "Use this PC to make and receive calls" is turned ON.
  5. Under the same section, look for the "Open calls in" option. Set it to "Phone" if available.
If calls still ring on your PC: Open Phone Link → Settings → Features → Calls and toggle the calls setting OFF then ON again. Then make a test call. If it still routes to your PC, try:
  1. Open Windows Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Phone Link
  2. Make sure your phone is listed and connected
  3. Restart both Phone Link on your PC and Link to Windows on your phone
Test it: Open Phone Link, go to the Calls tab, and dial any number. If the call rings on your actual phone (not your PC speakers), everything is set up correctly and Ask Dave Real Estate Tools's Dial button will work the same way.
Alternatives

If you don't want to use Phone Link

Phone Link is optional. Many agents in Dubai use WhatsApp for everything and skip phone calls entirely. If that's you:

  • Ignore the Dial button and use the WA button instead
  • You can also use the Dial button as a "Copy number" action – it copies the number to your clipboard so you can paste it into any other app or dial manually on your phone

iPhone + Windows BETA

Phone Link also works with iPhone via Bluetooth:

  1. Turn on Bluetooth on your iPhone.
  2. Open Phone Link on your PC and choose "iPhone".
  3. Scan the QR code with your iPhone camera and tap Pair.
  4. Grant permissions for calls, contacts, and notifications.

Calls go through your iPhone. Keep it within about 10 metres of your PC.

iPhone + Mac BETA

No extra apps needed – Apple handles this natively:

  1. Sign into the same Apple ID on both devices.
  2. Connect both to the same Wi-Fi network.
  3. On your iPhone: Settings → Phone → Calls on Other Devices → ON.
  4. Toggle on your Mac in the list below.

Android + Mac BETA

No built-in call routing for this combination. Use WhatsApp Desktop for all calls and messages instead.

4. The Panel – Templates & Contact Details

Panel

Your message template hub

Click the Ask Dave Real Estate Tools icon in your system tray to open the main panel. This is where you manage and customise your message scripts.

What's in the panel

  • Phone input: Type or paste a number at the top. Ask Dave Real Estate Tools normalises it automatically.
  • Contact card: Shows the active number with Dial and WhatsApp buttons, plus an optional name field.
  • Template list: All your saved message scripts. Click one to preview it.
  • Template preview: See the full message with the contact name filled in. Edit it if needed, then send via WhatsApp or copy to clipboard.

Using templates with contact names

Open panel → Number auto-fills from clipboard → Click "+ Add name" → Type "Ahmed" → Pick a template → "Hi Ahmed, ..." ready to send

Every template uses {name} as a placeholder. When you add a contact name, it replaces {name} throughout the message automatically.

Creating your own templates

  1. Open the panel (click the tray icon)
  2. Scroll to the bottom of the template list
  3. Click "+ New Template"
  4. Give it a name, pick a category, and write your message
  5. Use {name} anywhere you want the contact's name to appear
  6. Click Save

You can also edit or delete any template by hovering over it in the list.

Tip: Customise the built-in templates with your actual name and agency. Change "[Agent]" to your name and "[Agency]" to your firm. These templates are just starting points.

6. System Tray & Settings

System Tray

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools lives in your taskbar

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools runs in the background as a system tray icon. It starts automatically when you turn on your computer and stays out of your way until you need it.

ActionWhat happens
Left-click the tray iconOpens the main panel
Right-click the tray iconShows menu: Open Panel, Settings, Quit

Settings you can change

Right-click the tray icon and choose Settings:

  • Clipboard detection: Turn on/off automatic phone number detection
  • Global hotkeys: Turn on/off and customise keyboard shortcuts
    Default: Ctrl+Shift+D for Dial, Ctrl+Shift+W for WhatsApp
  • WhatsApp mode: Choose between opening WhatsApp in your browser ("Web") or in the WhatsApp Desktop app ("Desktop")
Which WhatsApp mode? If you have WhatsApp Desktop installed, use "Desktop" mode – it's faster and opens directly in the app. If you don't want to install it, "Web" mode opens wa.me links in your browser which works with WhatsApp Web.

Typical Workflow Examples

Example 1: Quick WhatsApp from CRM

  1. You're in your CRM looking at a lead's phone number
  2. Select the number and press Ctrl+C
  3. The popup appears next to your cursor
  4. Click WA → pick "Listing Introduction"
  5. WhatsApp opens with the message ready – just hit Send

Example 2: Follow-up with a personalised message

  1. Copy the client's phone number from anywhere
  2. Click the tray icon to open the panel
  3. The number is already there. Click "+ Add name" and type their name
  4. Pick the "Follow-Up" template
  5. The preview shows "Hi Ahmed, ..." with their name filled in
  6. Edit the message if needed, then click "Send via WhatsApp"

Example 3: Rapid calling through a list

  1. Open your contact list in Excel or your CRM
  2. Copy the first number – popup appears
  3. Click Dial – Phone Link opens the number on your phone
  4. Make your call, hang up
  5. Copy the next number – popup updates with the new number
  6. Click Dial again – repeat
