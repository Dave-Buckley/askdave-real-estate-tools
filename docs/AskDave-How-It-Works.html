Ask Dave Real Estate Tools – How It Works

A complete guide to every feature

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools v1.0.0 © 2026

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools runs quietly in your system tray and helps you do everything faster – dial, message on WhatsApp, send emails, take structured notes in OneNote, send RERA forms, book viewings, set follow-up reminders, and stay informed with UAE real estate news. It works on top of whatever you already use – your CRM, your browser, Excel, emails, PDFs – anything where you can highlight text on screen.

There are two main parts to the tool:

  • The Compact Window – a dark-themed frameless window (320×260) that appears when you highlight text and press Ctrl+Space
  • The Expanded Panel – the full-size view (380×680) with contact details, templates, forms, scheduling, and news

1. Smart Text Detection

Automatic

Highlight text, get instant actions

Highlight any text on your screen, then press Ctrl+Space. Ask Dave Real Estate Tools intelligently extracts phone numbers, email addresses, names, and property units from whatever you highlighted. The compact window appears with the detected information ready for action.

Highlight text on screen → Press Ctrl+Space → Compact window appears → Click Dial, WhatsApp, or Expand

Where it works: Everywhere. Browsers, CRM systems (Bayut, Property Finder, HubSpot, Salesforce), Excel, Word, PDFs, emails, WhatsApp Desktop, Outlook – if you can highlight text, Ask Dave Real Estate Tools will detect the data.

Email detection

Standalone email addresses are also detected from your highlighted text. When an email is found, it automatically enables the Gmail compose option in the expanded panel.

Block text extraction

You don't need to carefully select just a phone number. Highlight an entire CRM record, email signature, or contact card – the app will pull out all the fields automatically: phone number, email address, name, and property unit. Labels like "Full Name:", "Email:", "Property Interest:", and "Unit 1204, Marina Heights Tower" are all understood.

Smart number handling: Ask Dave Real Estate Tools automatically normalises every number to international format. Whether you highlight "0501234567" or "00971501234567" or "+971 50 123 4567", it all becomes the same clean number.

3. Click-to-Dial & Phone Link

Compact Window

How the Dial button works

When you click Dial in the compact window or the expanded panel, Ask Dave Real Estate Tools sends the phone number to your phone's dialler via Microsoft Phone Link. The number is pre-filled and ready – you still press the green call button yourself. It will not auto-call anyone.

Click Dial → Phone Link receives number → Your phone's dialler opens → You press Call
Setup

Setting up Phone Link (one-time, Android + Windows)

If you haven't connected your phone yet, follow these steps once:

  1. On your PC, open the Start menu and search for "Phone Link". Open it.
  2. On your Android phone, install "Link to Windows" from the Google Play Store.
  3. In Phone Link on your PC, click Get started. A QR code appears on screen.
  4. Open the Link to Windows app on your phone and scan the QR code.
  5. Grant all permissions when asked – calls, contacts, messages, and notifications.
Important

Making calls go to your phone (not your PC speakers)

By default, Phone Link may try to ring through your PC speakers instead of your phone. To make calls go straight to your phone's dialler:

  1. Open Phone Link on your PC.
  2. Click the Settings gear icon (top-right corner).
  3. Go to Features.
  4. Under Calls, make sure "Use this PC to make and receive calls" is turned ON.
  5. Under the same section, look for the "Open calls in" option. Set it to "Phone" if available.
If calls still ring on your PC: Open Phone Link → Settings → Features → Calls and toggle the calls setting OFF then ON again. Then make a test call. If it still routes to your PC, try:
  1. Open Windows Settings → Bluetooth & devices → Phone Link
  2. Make sure your phone is listed and connected
  3. Restart both Phone Link on your PC and Link to Windows on your phone
Test it: Open Phone Link, go to the Calls tab, and dial any number. If the call rings on your actual phone (not your PC speakers), everything is set up correctly and Ask Dave Real Estate Tools will work the same way.
Alternatives

If you don't want to use Phone Link

Phone Link is optional. Many agents in Dubai use WhatsApp for everything and skip phone calls entirely. If that's you:

  • Ignore the Dial button and use the WhatsApp button instead
  • You can also use the Dial button as a "Copy number" action – it copies the number to your clipboard so you can paste it into any other app or dial manually on your phone

iPhone + Windows BETA

Phone Link also works with iPhone via Bluetooth:

  1. Turn on Bluetooth on your iPhone.
  2. Open Phone Link on your PC and choose "iPhone".
  3. Scan the QR code with your iPhone camera and tap Pair.
  4. Grant permissions for calls, contacts, and notifications.

Calls go through your iPhone. Keep it within about 10 metres of your PC.

iPhone + Mac BETA

No extra apps needed – Apple handles this natively:

  1. Sign into the same Apple ID on both devices.
  2. Connect both to the same Wi-Fi network.
  3. On your iPhone: Settings → Phone → Calls on Other Devices → ON.
  4. Toggle on your Mac in the list below.

Android + Mac BETA

No built-in call routing for this combination. Use WhatsApp Desktop for all calls and messages instead.

4. The Expanded Panel – Full Feature View

Panel

Everything you need in one window

Click Expand in the compact window (or click the system tray icon) to open the full expanded panel. This is where all of the app's features live, laid out from top to bottom:

  1. Phone number input – The detected number appears here. You can also type or paste a number manually. The app normalises it automatically.
  2. Contact card – Shows editable fields for name, email, and unit/property. These are auto-filled from your highlighted text or can be entered manually.
  3. Action buttons (2×2 grid) – Dial, WhatsApp, Gmail, and OneNote. One click for each action.
  4. Schedule section – Book Viewing and Book Consultation buttons that open Google Calendar with event details pre-filled from the contact card.
  5. Follow-up reminders – 7-day, 15-day, and 30-day buttons. Creates a Google Calendar reminder with the contact's details.
  6. WhatsApp Templates – 6 message templates. Click one to send it via WhatsApp with placeholders filled in. Click the pencil icon to edit.
  7. Gmail Templates – 6 email templates. Click one to open Gmail compose with subject and body pre-filled. Click the pencil icon to edit.
  8. OneNote Templates – 5 role-based templates (Tenant, Landlord, Buyer, Seller, Investor). Creates a structured page in your "Real Estate" notebook with qualifying questions.
  9. Forms (Sales / Rentals / Off-plan tabs) – RERA contract forms. Send directly via WhatsApp or Gmail.
  10. KYC Forms – 3 internal compliance forms with a Download (Save As) button. For internal use only.
  11. UAE News Feed – Live headlines from 12 UAE real estate news sources. Click any headline to read the full article.

Using the panel with contact details

Highlight text → Ctrl+Space → Click Expand → Name, email, unit auto-filled → Pick any action

All templates use placeholders – {name}, {number}, {email}, and {unit} – which are automatically replaced with the contact's details from the contact card.

6. Additional Features

Panel

OneNote Integration

Click the OneNote button in the action grid, then select a client role (Tenant, Landlord, Buyer, Seller, or Investor). Ask Dave Real Estate Tools creates a structured page in a "Real Estate" notebook in your default OneNote notebooks folder, organised into sections by role.

Each page is pre-filled with the contact's details and role-specific qualifying questions – budget range, preferred locations, timeline, and other key information you need to capture during the conversation.

Requires: OneNote desktop application installed on your PC. The integration uses PowerShell COM automation to communicate with OneNote directly.
Pro tip – access your notes anywhere: Install the OneNote app on your phone and pin it to your pull-out edge panel or home screen. Your "Real Estate" notebook syncs automatically – so when a client calls, you can pull up their qualifying notes, property interests, and conversation history instantly, wherever you are.
Panel

Gmail Templates

The 6 Gmail templates open your browser with a Gmail compose window. The subject line and email body are pre-filled with the template content and the contact's details. This works by constructing a mailto: link – no Gmail login is needed within the app itself.

If an email address was detected from your highlighted text, it is automatically placed in the "To" field.

Panel

RERA Forms

The Forms section contains official RERA contract forms organised into three tabs:

  • Sales – Sale and purchase agreement forms
  • Rentals – Tenancy contract and rental forms
  • Off-plan – Off-plan sale and reservation forms

Each form can be sent directly to the client via WhatsApp or Gmail using the send buttons next to each form.

Panel

KYC Forms

The KYC Forms section contains 3 internal compliance forms used within your brokerage. These are download only – click the Save As button to download a copy to your computer. There is no send functionality for KYC forms, as they are meant for internal use.

Panel

Google Calendar – Book Viewings & Consultations

The Schedule section has two buttons: Book Viewing and Book Consultation. Clicking either one opens Google Calendar in your browser with a new event pre-filled with the contact's name, phone number, email, and property unit from the contact card.

You choose the date and time in Google Calendar, add any notes, and save. No Google account login is required within the app – it simply opens the calendar URL.

7. System Tray & Settings

System Tray

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools lives in your taskbar

Ask Dave Real Estate Tools runs in the background as a system tray icon. It starts automatically when you turn on your computer and stays out of your way until you need it.

Settings

Settings are accessible in two ways: right-click the tray icon and choose Settings, or click the gear icon in the expanded panel header.

  • Clipboard detection: Turn on/off automatic phone number detection
  • Hotkey customisation: Change the Ctrl+Space shortcut to a different key combination if needed
  • WhatsApp mode: Choose between opening WhatsApp in your browser ("Web") or in the WhatsApp Desktop app ("Desktop")
Which WhatsApp mode? If you have WhatsApp Desktop installed, use "Desktop" mode – it's faster and opens directly in the app. If you don't want to install it, "Web" mode opens wa.me links in your browser which works with WhatsApp Web.

Privacy & Security

  • No data leaves your computer. Ask Dave Real Estate Tools runs 100% locally. No cloud, no accounts, no tracking.
  • No CRM access. Ask Dave Real Estate Tools never connects to or reads from your CRM. It only sees text you highlight on screen.
  • Your templates are stored locally in your user profile. They stay on your machine.
  • No login required. The app has no authentication, no sign-up, and no user accounts. Your privacy is the default.
  • No hidden costs. Ask Dave Real Estate Tools is free to use. No subscriptions, no API fees, no trial periods.
